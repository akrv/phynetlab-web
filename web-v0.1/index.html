What is PhyNetLab?

Energy Neutral

PhyNetLab has 350 energy neutral nodes that are equipped with 1GHz band transceivers and 350 battery operated 2.4 GHz nodes. The nodes are available for experimentation with 6 programmable gateways at an industrial facility.

Remote Management

The Remote Management System provides multi-user management of the facility with API's to create senarios, program contexts and experiment strategies for modern IoT applications with energy as a context.

Data-as-a-Service

DaaS provides flexibility to your Applications and makes integration seamless. All IoT applications thrive on the data therefore, it is important that to have data instantly to create applications and actively iterate experiments.

Publication

PhyNode: An intelligent, cyber-physical system with energy neutral operation for PhyNetLab

Abstract

This paper introduces an ultra-low power cyber- physical system with energy harvesting capabilities called the PhyNode. This hardware is used for testing and deploying low power wireless sensor networks focused on real time material handling systems. Physical Internet Laboratory (PhyNetLab) is a materials handling facility focussed on researching various use cases of Physical Internet deployments in the field of logistics. PhyNode has a TI MSP430FR5969 with a sub-1Ghz band transceiver powered with a battery and sports solar cell for energy harvesting which is the main focus of the conducted research on PhyNode. The hardware platform also has a TI CC2530 System on Chip with 2.4Ghz transceiver that supports IEEE 802.15.4, Zigbee and RF4CE applications and implements a controller network supporting experimentation with 350 nodes. This paper outlines the system description of such hardware and in addition to the system design description with testbed deployment and network implementation; a comparison between such existing platforms is also presented in this paper.

PhyNetLab: Architecture Design of Ultra-Low Power Wireless Sensor Network Testbed

Abstract

PhyNetLab is a Wireless Sensor Network (WSN) Testbed for conducting research on ultra low power electronics for WSN, energy harvesting, energy neutral devices, suitable communication protocols and software strategies focussing on im- plementations in the field of materials handling and warehousing. The interactions between machines, handling systems and storage facilities is highly physical; to facilitate such interactions of various entities a PHYsical interNET is required. Henceforth such a WSN testbed is named as PhyNetLab. This article introduces aforementioned facility and further describes its architecture design, network topology and data flow for the nodes and a facilitation tool with state of the art techniques to easily deploy, experiment and validate various test scenarios. PhyNetLab design is also intended to be a model for WSN implementation for Industry 4.0 standard.
